# Tiny Heroes RPG – Game Design Document

## 1. Overview
**Tiny Heroes RPG** is a simple tabletop role-playing game designed for small children (approx. ages 4–8). The game focuses on imagination, cooperation, and fun rather than rules mastery. Rolling dice should feel exciting, characters are easy to understand, and failure is never harsh—often funny instead.

Core goals:
- Very simple rules
- Rolling high numbers is fun and celebrated
- Only four easy-to-understand skills
- Encourages creativity, teamwork, and storytelling

---

## 2. Core Design Principles

1. **Simplicity First**
   - Minimal math (small numbers only)
   - One core dice mechanic for almost everything

2. **Positive Play**
   - Success is common
   - Failure leads to funny or interesting outcomes, not punishment

3. **Exciting Dice**
   - Rolling high numbers always feels good
   - Rolling a 6 is special

4. **Fast Play**
   - Characters fit on one page
   - Rules can be explained in 5 minutes

---

## 3. Materials Needed
- One six-sided die (d6) per player
- Character sheets (one page)
- Pencils / crayons
- Optional: tokens or hearts for health

---

## 4. Characters

Each player controls one **Hero**.

### 4.1 Skills
Heroes have **four skills**, each rated from **0 to 3**.

- **Strong Stuff**
  - Climbing, jumping, lifting, fighting, breaking things

- **Smart Stuff**
  - Solving puzzles, remembering facts, noticing clues

- **Sneaky Stuff**
  - Hiding, sneaking, running fast, dodging

- **Kind Stuff**
  - Talking, making friends, helping others, calming animals

Skill ratings:
- 0 = Not good yet
- 1 = Beginner
- 2 = Trained
- 3 = Expert

---

## 5. Core Dice Mechanic

Whenever a hero tries something risky or important, they make a **Skill Roll**.

### 5.1 How to Roll
1. Roll **1d6**
2. Add the relevant **skill number**
3. Compare the total to the **Target Number (TN)**

**If you roll a 6 on the die, roll again and add it!**
(This can happen multiple times.)

---

## 6. Difficulty Levels

The Game Guide (GM) chooses one of four difficulty levels:

- **Easy (TN 4)** – everyday actions
- **Normal (TN 6)** – common challenges
- **Hard (TN 8)** – tricky or dangerous tasks
- **Epic (TN 10+)** – legendary or heroic feats

Higher totals mean better results.

---

## 7. Special Dice Results

### 7.1 Rolling a 6 – Critical Success
- Rolling a 6 is always exciting
- Something extra good happens
- Examples:
  - Do it faster
  - Help a friend too
  - Look extra cool

### 7.2 Rolling a 1 – Fumble
- Rolling a 1 never stops the story
- Something silly, noisy, or inconvenient happens
- Examples:
  - You slip but catch yourself
  - You make a funny noise
  - You drop something (but can pick it up)

---

## 8. Health & Danger

Heroes are tough and brave.

- Each hero starts with **3 Hearts** ❤️❤️❤️
- Losing all hearts means the hero is **tired or scared**, not hurt badly
- Resting, helping each other, or story events restore hearts

No character ever dies.

---

## 9. Cooperation & Teamwork

Heroes are encouraged to help each other:
- A hero can explain how they help
- The GM may give **+1** to the roll for good teamwork

Helping is more important than winning.

---

## 10. The Game Guide (GM)

The GM:
- Describes the world and the problems
- Chooses difficulty levels
- Decides fun outcomes for success and failure

Guidelines for GMs:
- Say "yes" often
- Keep things moving
- Make failures funny, not frustrating
- Let kids solve problems creatively

---

## 11. Example Skill Roll

Lina wants to climb a wall.
- The GM says it is **Normal (TN 6)**
- Lina has **Strong Stuff 2**
- She rolls a **6** → rolls again → gets a **3**
- Total = 6 + 3 + 2 = **11**

Epic success! Lina climbs easily and helps a friend up.

---

## 12. Optional Rules

### 12.1 Special Items
- Pets, magic hats, shiny tools
- Give +1 in special situations

### 12.2 Story Rewards
- After an adventure, heroes may increase **one skill by 1** (max 3)

---

## 13. Tone & Theme

Tiny Heroes RPG is:
- Bright
- Friendly
- Funny
- Adventurous

The game is about being brave, kind, and creative together.

---

## 14. End of Document

This document defines the complete ruleset for **Tiny Heroes RPG**. The game should always favor fun, imagination, and shared storytelling over strict rules.
